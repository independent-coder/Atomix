What is Atomix?

Atomix is an interactive periodic table of elements built with modern web technologies. It allows you to explore all 118 chemical elements with real-time visualizations, comprehensive data, and intuitive interactions. Features include color-coded element categories, advanced search and filtering, interactive hover tooltips with detailed information, and direct Wikipedia links for deeper learning. The application also includes a comprehensive chemistry glossary covering element categories, atomic properties, and fundamental chemical concepts. Perfect for students, educators, and science enthusiasts, Atomix makes learning chemistry engaging and accessible with its sleek dark theme design and fully responsive layout.

Chemistry Glossary

Element Categories

Alkali Metals
Group 1 elements, highly reactive, soft metals
Alkaline Earth Metals
Group 2 elements, reactive but less than alkali metals
Transition Metals
Groups 3-12, typical metallic properties, form colored compounds
Metalloids
Elements with properties intermediate between metals and nonmetals
Post-Transition Metals
Metallic elements located to the right of transition metals
Nonmetals
Elements lacking metallic properties, poor conductors
Halogens
Group 17 elements, highly reactive nonmetals
Noble Gases
Group 18 elements, chemically inert under normal conditions
Lanthanides
Elements 57-71, f-block, similar chemical properties
Actinides
Elements 89-103, f-block, all radioactive

Atomic Properties

Atomic Number
Number of protons in an atom's nucleus
Atomic Mass
Total mass of protons, neutrons, and electrons in an atom
Period
Horizontal row in the periodic table
Group
Vertical column in the periodic table
Valence Electrons
Electrons in the outermost shell, determine chemical reactivity
Electronegativity
Tendency of an atom to attract electrons in a chemical bond
Ionization Energy
Energy required to remove an electron from an atom

Chemical Concepts

Element
Pure substance consisting of only one type of atom
Compound
Substance formed from two or more elements chemically bonded
Molecule
Group of atoms bonded together, smallest unit of a compound
Isotope
Atoms of same element with different numbers of neutrons
Ion
Atom or molecule with electrical charge due to gain/loss of electrons
Chemical Bond
Attraction between atoms that enables formation of chemical substances
Periodic Law
Properties of elements repeat periodically with increasing atomic number
